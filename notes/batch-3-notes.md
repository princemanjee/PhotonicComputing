## Source 5: https://spie.org/news/history-and-technology-of-wavelength-division-multiplexing
**Title**: History and technology of wavelength division multiplexing
**Author/Publisher**: Frederic Su (interviewer); Jean-Pierre Laude (interviewee); SPIE, published in OE Reports Number 166
**Date**: 30 October 1997
**Status**: FETCHED
**Key Claims**:
- Carrier-wave frequencies span 192 to 196 THz.
- FDM vs. WDM terminology threshold: frequency spacing less than 50 GHz (0.4 nm) is called FDM; above is WDM.
- Grating groove density: several tens to several thousands of grooves per millimeter.
- IBM Muxmaster transmits 20 wavelengths (10 in each direction) at up to 2 Gbits/s each.
- Optical bandwidth per 1300-nm and 1550-nm window: approximately 15,000 GHz each.
- Theoretical channel capacity: 3,000 channels at 0.03 nm spacing.
- Current (1997) commercial minimum channel spacing: 0.4 nm (50 GHz).
- Published minimum channel spacing at time of interview: 0.1 nm.
- Demonstrated grating router: 25 input to 26 output ports at 0.8-nm spacing; feasibility shown for 157 input to 157 output ports at 0.05-nm spacing.
- Current manufacturing capacity at Jobin Yvon: 131-channel WDMs.
- Practical laser-source limit at time: up to 32 wavelengths at 100- or 50-GHz spacing.
- ITU standardization proposal: 100 GHz spacing (approximately 0.8 nm).
- TDM utility bit-rate threshold: less than 2 Mbits/s.
- Uncompressed HDTV bandwidth requirement: approximately 2 Gbits/s.
- Uncertainty relationship limit at 5-Gbits/s bit rate: approximately 5-GHz optical frequency spacing floor.
**Named Entities**:
- Technologies: WDM, FDM, TDM, Stimax configuration, arrayed waveguide gratings, fiber Bragg gratings, Michelson echelon gratings.
- Products: IBM Muxmaster; Stimax (Jobin Yvon).
- Researchers: R. T. Denton, T. S. Kinsel, Takahashi, Dragone, Jean-Pierre Laude.
- Companies/Institutions: Jobin Yvon; Instruments SA; Lucent; NEC; 3M; Centre National d'Etudes des Telecommunications (Lannion, France); Ecole Superieure d'Optique (ESO); University of Paris; Institut National des Telecommunications.
- Standards body: International Telecommunication Union (ITU).
**Direct Quotes**:
- "the bit rate can be increased further by using several carrier waves that are propagating without significant interaction on the same cable"
- "With 5-Gbits/s bit rate, the uncertainty relationship gives about 5-GHz limit for optical frequency spacing"
- "Three-thousand-channel classical grating spectrometers are selling readily, so why not WDMs?"
**Notes**:
- Cited historical milestones: 1958 IEEE paper by R. T. Denton and T. S. Kinsel on optical multiplexing concept; 1967 holographic grating development at Jobin Yvon; 1973 Summer School in Electromagnetism at CNET Lannion; circa 1990 arrayed waveguide grating proposals by Takahashi (Japan) and Dragone (U.S.).
